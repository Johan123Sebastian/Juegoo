Learn Jungle Vocabulary

Welcome to the jungle! Here you will learn essential vocabulary related to this fascinating ecosystem.

Animals

  • Monkey: An agile mammal that lives in trees and is known for its intelligence.
  • Tiger: A large feline that is an agile and powerful hunter, a symbol of the jungle.
  • Jaguar: A large feline known for its stealth and power.
  • Bird: A flying animal found in various species and colors in the jungle.
  • Anaconda: A giant snake found in rivers and swamps of the jungle.
  • Frog: An amphibian that lives near bodies of water and is known for its croaking.
  • Butterfly: A colorful insect that flies among the flowers of the jungle.
  • Crocodile: A large reptile that lives in rivers and is known for its ferocity.
  • Capybara: The largest rodent in the world, often found near water.
  • Sloth: A slow-moving mammal that spends most of its life in trees.

Plants

  • Tree: A tall plant that provides shade and habitat for many animals.
  • Orchid: A plant with exotic flowers that grows in the jungle, known for its beauty.
  • Palm Tree: A tall plant with large leaves that grows in warm climates.
  • Bamboo: A fast-growing plant used for many purposes.
  • Vine: A climbing plant that can wrap around trees and other structures.
  • Fern: A plant that reproduces through spores and is commonly found in humid areas.
  • Cacao: A tree whose seeds are used to make chocolate.
  • Hibiscus: A flowering plant known for its large, colorful blooms.
  • Mahogany: A hardwood tree valued for its timber.
  • Ceiba: A large tree that can reach great heights and has a distinctive trunk.

Phenomena

  • River: A body of freshwater that flows through the jungle, home to many aquatic species.
  • Waterfall: A fall of water from a great height that creates an impressive landscape.
  • Flood: An overflow of water that submerges land, often occurring during heavy rain.
  • Storm: A disturbance in the atmosphere that can bring heavy rain, wind, and thunder.
  • Lightning: A natural electrical discharge that occurs during storms.
  • Fog: A thick cloud of tiny water droplets suspended in the air near the ground.
  • Sunlight: The natural light from the sun that is vital for plant growth.
  • Night: The period of darkness in each twenty-four hours when the sun is below the horizon.
  • Earthquake: A sudden and violent shaking of the ground, often caused by tectonic shifts.
  • Wildfire: An uncontrolled fire that spreads rapidly through the vegetation in the jungle.
